Exploring Social Security policy options

Using Urban Institute’s Dynamic Simulation of Income Model, we can explore the distribution of income, wealth, and Social Security, by year and population group, under various policy scenarios.

  • Per capita scenarios show outcomes on an individual basis.
  • Equivalent scenarios adjust the per capita figures to reflect the idea that larger households can pool their resources to live more economically than a single-person household.
  • “Current law scheduled” assumes that Social Security benefits are paid out as promised, even after the trust fund runs out.
  • “Current law payable” assumes that Social Security benefits are reduced by a uniform amount after the trust fund runs out so that all benefits in each year can be paid out of revenues from that year.
